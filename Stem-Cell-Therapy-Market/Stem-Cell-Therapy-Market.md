<p>The Japan Stem Cell Therapy Market is segmented into product type, which is each having varied therapeutic potential. In this segment, Neuronal Stem Cells have also been highlighted because of their application in the treatment of neurological disorders such as Parkinson's disease and spinal cord damage. Adipose-derived Stem Cells, which are isolated from fat tissue, are used in wound healing, tissue repair, and orthopedic conditions. Hematopoietic Stem Cells (HSCs) are crucial in the treatment of blood-related diseases like leukemia due to their capacity to regenerate blood cells. Dedifferentiated Fat (DFAT) Cells, being adipose-derived stem cells, possess greater regenerative ability, especially in bone and tissue regeneration.</p>
<p>Along with ASCs, Very Small Embryonic Like Stem Cells (VSELs) present in adult tissue are another element of the market that are pluripotent in nature. Pluripotent and capable of differentiation into all cell types are Human Embryonic Stem Cells (hESCs), but in controversy as to their use, being questionable considering ethical reasons. Finally, Induced Pluripotent Stem Cells (iPSCs), derived from reprogrammed adult cells, possess the same characteristics as embryonic stem cells and are researched for personalized medicine and body repair.</p>

<h4><strong>By Technology</strong></h4>
<p>The market for stem cell therapy is also categorized by technology, as there are many advanced processes that lead to the development and use of stem cell therapies. Cell Acquisition encompasses the techniques of acquiring stem cells for their use in therapy. Apheresis is an operation in which the stem cells are isolated from the blood and are usually applied in collecting the hematopoietic stem cells for leukemia. Bone Marrow Harvest is a prime procedure as well, whereby stem cells are retrieved from bone marrow and often for treatments for the blood. Umbilical Blood Cord comprises of blood collected from umbilical cord blood that consists of many hematopoietic stem cells with prospects to heal disorders related to the blood as well as for the regeneration of other tissues.</p>
<p>Cryopreservation is an important technology employed in the preservation of stem cells to be used later. Cryopreservation is where stem cells are frozen at extreme low temperatures and kept viable even after a significant period. Cryopreservation keeps the stem cells alive and ready to use for therapeutics when in need, although there may be a time interval between collection and treatment.</p>
<p>Cell Production involves technologies employed to produce high amounts of stem cells for clinical use. Therapeutic Cloning entails the production of an embryo specifically to harvest stem cells with identical genetic material to that of the patient, aiding in minimizing immune rejection. Cell Culture is the method of cultivating stem cells in controlled conditions to increase their numbers. In-Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is also a method that can be employed to create embryos, where stem cells may be obtained for treatment purposes. Isolation describes the removal of stem cell populations from an admixed cell culture to retrieve the best possible cells for therapy.</p>
<p>Lastly, Expansion and Sub-Culture are key methods of propagating stem cells and keeping them competent to differentiate into desired cell types. These procedures are vital to produce adequate amounts of stem cells to address clinical needs, making treatments scalable and effective.</p>
<h4><strong>By Therapy</strong></h4>
<p>The Japan Stem Cell Therapy Market is also classified based on therapy type, primarily Allogenic Stem Cell Therapy and Autologous Stem Cell Therapy, each having different methods of treatment.</p>
<p>Allogenic Stem Cell Therapy is the process of using stem cells from a donor, as opposed to the patient themselves. The stem cells are normally derived from such sources as bone marrow, umbilical cord blood, or peripheral blood. The therapy turns out to be very effective for the treatment of diseases like hematologic diseases, such as leukemia, where cells of the patient are damaged or non-functional. One of the greatest strengths of allogenic therapy is that it is possible to give it to patients who lack their own viable stem cells, meaning more patients become candidates for stem cell therapy. There are some disadvantages, however, including immune rejection and graft-versus-host disease, happening when the recipient's immune system identifies the donor cells as foreign. Advances in immunosuppressive therapy and improved matching of donor cells are making such problems manageable.</p>
<p>On the contrary, Autologous Stem Cell Therapy requires the use of stem cells of the patient himself. These stem cells are commonly harvested from donors such as adipose tissue, bone marrow, or peripheral blood and processed prior to being infused into the body of the patient. One of the biggest advantages of autologous therapy is that there is no risk of immune rejection because the stem cells belong to the patient. This makes the therapy safer and simpler in most cases. Autologous stem cell therapy is also commonly used in treatments for orthopedic injuries, cardiac regeneration, and autoimmune diseases. The drawback, though, is that some patients may not have sufficient healthy stem cells to be effectively utilized, which makes it less suitable in certain cases.</p>
